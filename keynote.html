What is Ninja Robot?

ニンジャで何ですか

🎨

Learn STEAM Through Play （遊びながらSTEAMを学ぶ）

Turn coding and engineering into fun, hands-on adventures. Build and program a real robot to grasp complex ideas naturally.

🛠️

More Than a Toy—A Creation Tool （おもちゃ以上、創造のツール）

Not just instructions. Ninja Robot is a platform to design, build, and code your own unique projects.

🧠

A Friend with an AI Brain （AI頭脳を持つ友達）

Powered by Google Gemini AI. Talk to it, give commands, and learn together through natural interaction.

🌍

Open Source, Globally Connected （オープンソースで世界とつながる）

Rooted in open-source spirit. Connected with Otto DIY—the world’s largest robot learning community.

Expandable Ninja Robot Module and Design

拡張可能なモジュールとAI統合機能

🔩

Hardware

The Body and Brain
体と脳

💻

Software

The Soul and Skills
コントロールと機能

  • Raspberry Pi Family: The powerful, compact brain of the robot, compatible with various models like the Pi Zero 2W for ultimate flexibility.
  • Customized Dev Board: Simplifies connections for sensors, motors, and power, making assembly and expansion a breeze.
  • Customizable Modules: Easily add new sensors for sight, sound, and touch, or attach new parts to create a truly unique robot.
  • Gemini Robot Agent: The AI soul that allows you to control the robot with natural language, ask questions, and give it a personality.
  • Python Robot Drive: The core open-source software that controls the robot's movements and functions, perfect for learning and modification.
  • GitHub Repository: Access all our code and designs. Learn from the community, contribute your own ideas, and help build the future of Ninja Robot together.

Why Ninja Robot?

なぜニンジャ　ロボット？

1. Supporting Existing Education System

（既存の教育システムを支援）

Ninja Robot lowers barriers for teachers, making STEAM easier to introduce in any classroom.

2. From Test-Prep to Creativity

（テスト勉強から創造へ）

Shifts focus from rote memorization to hands-on projects that inspire imagination.

3. Bringing STEAM to Life

（STEAMを体感に）

Turns abstract ideas like coding into playful, tangible experiences students can grasp.
